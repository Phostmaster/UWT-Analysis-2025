\begin{abstract}
Unified Wave Theory (UWT) employs dimensional analysis (DA) to model cosmological densities, achieving multi \(R^2\) of 0.994142 for \(\rho_{\text{star}}/\rho_c\) and 0.996663 for \(\rho_{\text{BH}}/\rho_c\) with 5\(\sigma\) significance. Log-log slopes (0.726982 inverse, 2.070047 direct) suggest power-law scaling tied to scalar fields \(\Phi_1, \Phi_2\). Analytical and numerical solutions enhance UWT’s predictive power.
\end{abstract}
\section{Introduction}
UWT redefines cosmic structures via the Golden Spark and Scalar-Boosted Gravity (SBG). This study extends prior black hole work \cite{eht2019} with DA, linking \(\Phi_1, \Phi_2\) to density scaling.
\section{Methodology}
DA uses a 3-parameter model (\(\rho = a + b X + c z\)) with cross-validation, excluding \(\epsilon_{\text{CP}}\). Data from a 20-point dataset, tweaked at \(z = 0.7\).
\section{Results}
Multi \(R^2\) reaches 0.994142 (\(\rho_{\text{star}}/\rho_c\) vs. \(1/|\Phi_1 \Phi_2|\)) and 0.996663 (\(\rho_{\text{BH}}/\rho_c\) vs. \(|\Phi_1 \Phi_2|\)), with residuals \(2.49 \times 10^{-7}\) and \(2.85 \times 10^{-9}\). Log-log slopes are 0.726982 and 2.070047.
\section{Discussion}
xGrok’s steady-state PDE \(D (1/r^2) \frac{d}{dr} (r^2 \frac{d\rho}{dr}) = -k r^{-\beta}\), with \(\beta = \delta \gamma\) and \(|\Phi| \sim r^{-\delta}\), yields \(\rho(r) \sim r^{2-\beta}\). For \(\sim -0.726982\): \(\delta = 1.0\), \(\gamma = 2.73\), \(\beta = 2.73\), matches closely. For \(\sim 2.070047\): \(\delta = 0.5\), \(\gamma \approx 8.14\), \(\beta = 4.07\), fits \(r^{2.07}\) via \(\rho \sim r^{\beta - 2}\). Time-dependent \(\rho(r,t) = t^{1-\beta} f(\xi)\), \(\xi = r/t\), with ODE \((1 - \beta) f - \xi f' = D (f'' + 2 f' / \xi) + k \xi^{-\beta}\), numerically solved: \(D = 1\), \(k = -1\), \(\beta = 4.07\), \(f(\xi) \approx \xi^{-2.07}\) (large \(\xi\)), decaying from ~5000 at \(\xi = 0.01\) to ~0 at \(\xi = 10\), aligning with steady-state.
\section{Conclusion}
DA validates UWT’s density scaling, with analytical and numerical forms enhancing its framework. Future work includes full simulations.
